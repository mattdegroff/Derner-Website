Welcome to
The Progress Project!

We are very excited to hear about your experiences in therapy! We can’t do this without you!

The Progress Project is a collaboration among clinicians, patients, and researchers. We collect data from patients and therapists in a wide range of private practices and mental health clinics. The Progress Project is a collaboration between researchers, clinicians, and patients around the globe. Our main research team is based at Adelphi University, New York. Our goals are to learn about patients’ progress in therapy, understand what makes psychotherapy work, and develop ways to improve treatments and clinical training.


The Team

His research focuses on understanding how individuals change in the process of psychotherapy and investigating what makes specific treatments work for specific individuals. Dr. Aafjes-van Doorn is also a licensed clinical psychologist with clinical experience in individual and group therapy using different therapy models in the Netherlands, the UK and the USA.

Patient Information

We’re glad that you are interested in The Progress Project.

What is The Progress Project?

Our goal is to understand how people like you progress over the course of their therapy/counseling sessions. We are interested in your experience in therapy and curious about things you find more helpful or beneficial.

If you choose to join our project, you will be asked to fill out several questionnaires on a regular basis to help us assess your progress over time (more details on that just below).

How much time will it take?

  • When you enroll for the first time: We will ask you a series of questions, which will take you about 15 minutes.
  • While you are in therapy/counseling: You could choose the option that fits you best:
    1. 5 minutes, once a week: Complete a very brief weekly online survey (5 minutes) after your weekly therapy/counseling session
      OR
    2. 20 minutes, once a month: Complete a longer monthly online survey.
  • 10 minutes every 3 months after therapy ended: Complete a 10-minute online survey 1, 3, 6, and 12 months after your therapy ended.

You don’t have to remember when to go online. If you want you can opt-in to receive email reminders.

Can I opt-out of the project at any point?

Yes, absolutely, you can opt-out of the project at any point.

Why Should I Participate?

  • Help you.The online surveys could help you take a few minutes once a week or once a month to reflect on your experiences in therapy. It might help you to get a better sense of how therapy might be helping you and what areas you are struggling with.

  • Help others.
    Your participation in the project is a significant contribution to the development of better treatments that could benefit other individuals who will be attending therapy/counseling in the future. Your answers will help us produce research that has real-life implications and could make a difference in people’s lives.

  • You might get lucky.
    Each time you complete the online survey, you can choose to enter a bimonthly raffle to win a $50 Amazon gift card.

Will your information be kept safe?

  • We are committed to keeping your information completely confidential – we do not store your data with any of your identifying information and we do not share it with anyone outside of the research team.
  • For more details on the steps we are taking to make sure your information and IP address is kept safe, click here
  • Your therapist/counselor will not know that you participate in The Progress Project, unless, you decide to share this information yourself.

We'd love to hear from you!

  • If you have any questions, ideas or concerns about the project, please contact us at info@myprogressproject.com
  • If you worry about your mental health, you can speak to your therapist/counselor or you may call the national helpline, 1-800-273-TALK (8255), where trained counselors can offer support and/or help you find local community agencies.

Therapist Information

Welcome to The Progress Project!

We invite you to join this collaborative initiative of researchers, clinicians, and patients to increase our understanding of what makes therapy work.

Why should you join us? How does the project benefit you?

  • Reflect on your work.
    The process of answering our questions could help you reflect on your experience.

  • Being part of a community of professionals.
    Through your participation in the project you will become a part of a network of clinicians and researchers who are interested in developing research that helps understand therapeutic processes that occur in real clinical practice.We would be delighted to collaborate with you if you would like to get more involved in the research we are conducting at The Progress Project.

  • Help more patients.
    Our responses to our questions will help us understand patterns that occur in psychotherapy, and hopefully produce research that will lead to development of better treatments in the future.

  • Building an evidence-base for psychotherapy.
    In the long-term your participation may be beneficial to you in that over the long-term the data collected and resulting publications, may assist with attracting 3rd party funding for psychological services.

How to participate in The Progress Project

Complete a one-time survey: This will take only 10 minutes of your time. We will ask you (de-identified) demographic information and a series of questions about your experience in therapy.

Get your patients involved in the project!

If your patients opt to participate in the study, they will be asked to complete an initial online survey (15 minutes), followed by a 5-minute weekly or a 20-minute monthly survey throughout their time in therapy. Their participation is completely anonymous and we will not be able to share with you whether or not they chose to participate.

Please invite your patients to sign up via www.myprogressproject.com.

To make it easier for you to spread the word and share the information about The Progress Project with your patients, we have drafted an example email message that you can use or individualize to send to patients.

(Click the letter to open in an email application)

As I briefly mentioned in our therapy session today, The Progress Project is a collaborative online research study assessing process and outcome of therapy/counseling. This online survey platform allows you to monitor your progress throughout the therapy process.

All you need to do is click here (http://www.myprogressproject.com) and enter “Patient Survey” at the top of the home page. You will be asked to complete a 15-minute initial survey, followed by brief (5-minute) weekly or longer (20-minute) monthly surveys. If you want, the team can send you email reminders when to fill out the online surveys.

Your participation in the study could help you reflect on your experiences in therapy. It will also provide an important contribution to clinical research that will enable a better understanding of what makes therapy work. Participation is free, anonymous, and confidential.

Thank you,

Dr. XX

Frequently Asked Questions

General Info

What is The Progress Project about?

As researchers in The Progress in Mental Health Project, we try to get a better understanding of the experience of therapists and patients in the real world. We would like to know what characterizes therapists in the field and how patients progress over the course of psychotherapy. We like to know what therapy/counseling sessions are really like, about the patient-therapist relationship and what kind of difficulties get better over time.

Knowing more about your psychotherapy experiences will provide valuable information on what really happens in clinical practice and what types of therapies work best for whom. This will give us ideas about how we might be able to improve the psychotherapy/ counseling that is available, so that in future therapists can be better trained and more patients can benefit.

Who runs and funds The Progress Project?

The Progress Project is an initiative based on collaboration among therapists, patients and researchers who are interested in advancing clinically-relevant research with potential practical implications. The research team is led by Dr. Jacques Barber, a world-known psychotherapy researcher and includes multiple junior and senior clinician-researchers. For more information on the core team, please click here.

The Progress Project is not funded by any governmental or private agencies and is based completely on the voluntary participation of researchers, therapists and patients. We would greatly appreciate any support. For donations, please contact info@myprogressproject.com.

What do I need to do to get involved?

Just click on “patient survey” if you are a patient, or “therapist survey” if you are a therapist.

When you enter the online survey we'll show you the study information and consent form. Then we will ask you some demographic questions, and questions about your interpersonal and emotional experiences.

If you are a therapist, we will ask you to fill out this online survey once. It won't take longer than 10 minutes of your time.

If you are a patient, we will ask you to complete an initial online survey (15 minutes), and then complete regular brief online surveys throughout your therapy treatment.

What questionnaires do you use on the patient and therapist survey?

The following list sets out the assessment measures currently used as part of The Progress Project.

  • The Cooper-Norcross Inventory of Preferences (CNIP: Cooper & Norcross, 2016)
  • Treatment Outcome Package (TOP; Kraus, Seligman, & Jordan, 2005).
  • Comprehensive Measure of Psychotherapy Outcome (COMPO; Chui et al., in preparation).
  • Reflective Functioning Questionnaire (RF; Fonagy et al., 2016).
  • Multitheoretical List of Therapeutic Interventions - Short (MULTI-Short; McCarthy & Barber, 2009).
  • The Working Alliance Inventory-Short Form (Tracey & Kokotovic, 1989)
  • The Personality Inventory for DSM-V (PID-5; Krueger, Derringer, Markon, Watson & Skodol, 2013)
  • The Experiences in Close Relationships-Relationship Structures Questionnaire (ECR-RS; Fraley, Heffernan, Vicary, & Brumbaugh, 2011)

The measures that we use to track progress in therapy are not perfect.  Sometimes people report negative change on outcome measures when in fact their symptoms improved or people find that the questions don’t really reflect their experiences.  Likewise, online surveys can never describe the rich complexities of growth and healing in therapy.  Given these limitations, we still think it is important to track your outcomes with these measures. They are one important source of information regarding your progress or deterioration, and can be helpful when you want to reflect on or discuss your experiences with your therapist.

If there is a measure that you would like to see included, please contact us at info@myprogressproject.com

Patient Info

Do we get session-by-session feedback?

Unfortunately, we currently do not have sufficient funding to provide feedback using our data. We hope that in the future this would be made possible. To receive updates, please contact us at info@myprogressproject.com

Does my therapist get to see my results on the online surveys?

Your participation is completely confidential. Whether or not you participate in the surveys will not be known by your therapist unless you tell him/her. Your responses will not be shared with your therapist or any 3rd party agency.

Can I participate if I live outside the USA?

Yes of course, the benefit of an online survey is that you can complete it anywhere anytime. Data from different countries will be very valuable and will allow us to compare treatments in different settings and different countries.

Can I participate even if I have been in therapy for a long time or if I just started?

Absolutely! As long as you’ve attended at least one therapy session you are welcome to participate.
